Bangkok

When you go to Bangkok you must see the Grand Palace and
Wat Prakaew. for 150 years the home of Thai Kings and the
Royal court continues to have a lot of visitors because it
has the most beautiful architecture.

The Wat Arun's towering spires is one of the most recognized
in Southeast Asia, it has a lot of patterns, it also symbolizes
the birth of the Rattanakosin Period.

Kuala Lumpur

Kuala Lumpur is recognised for boasting gleaming
skyscrapers, colonial architecture, charming locals, and a
myriad of natural attractions.

Hailed as the Twin Jewels of Kuala Lumpur, a visit to KL
just is not complete unless you have visited these doppelganger
structures.

At the heart of Kuala Lumpur is an area which never sleeps,
far more colourful and bustling than its bigger and more
glamourous neighbours. it is the famous China Town, also
known as 'Chee Cheong Kai' (Starch Factory Street), a reference
to its roots as a tapioca-producing district.

Batu Caves is a limestone hill comprising three major caves
and smaller ones. Located in the north, this 100-year-old temple
features idols and statues erected inside

Bali

Bali is full of wonderful places you must see we suggest:

Tanah Lot temp is the island's most iconic temple. This temple
is perched on top of a huge rock, and surrounded by the sea.
As among Bali's most important sea temples, Tanah Lot pays
homage to the guardian spirits of the sea. The temple serves
as foreground to the most picturesque sunsets.

The Ubud Art Market, referred to as 'PasarSeni Ubud' is
located opposite the the Puri Saren Royal UbudPal. Here you
can find beautiful silk scarves and many other hand-crafted goods.
